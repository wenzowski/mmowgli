Turn the tide in Somalia!

Solving the problem of piracy in Somalia is a mission almost as challenging as turning a natural tide in the sea.

  • The Somalia coast is as long as the US coast from Maine to Florida -- a lot to patrol.
  • The merchant fleets of the world would rather carry insurance to pay off pirate ransoms than engage them in kinetic action.
  • Those same fleets often fly the "flags for a fee" -- flags of nations that essentially sell the right to fly their flags, making it illegal for other nations' navies to board the ships.
  • The pirates have extended their range by capturing large merchant ships and using them as "motherships" that serve as sea stations across the Indian Ocean.
  • The communications infrastructure is weak.
