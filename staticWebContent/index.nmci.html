Turn the tide in Somalia!

Solving the problem of piracy in Somalia has been a challenging mission throughout modern times.

  • The Somalia coast is as long as the US coast from Maine to Florida -- there is a lot of ocean to patrol.
  • The merchant fleets of the world would rather carry insurance to pay off pirate ransoms than engage in hostilities.
  • Those same fleets often fly the "flags for a fee" -- flags of nations that essentially sell the right to fly their flags, making it illegal for other nations' navies to board the ships.
  • The pirates have extended their range by capturing large merchant ships and using them as "motherships" that serve as sea stations across the Indian Ocean.
  • Because the distances are so large, the communications infrastructure is weak.

These are just some of the sea-based problems. Other causes of Somalia piracy are actually land-based problems:

  • Somalia is a failed state. It has no functional central government.
  • Somalia has no legal infrastructure for incarcerating pirates or even having them stand trial.
  • Somalia has a limited economy: piracy is often seen as the only path to "upward mobility."
  • The Somali people are not a single people -- they are a loose affiliation of many nomadic clans.
